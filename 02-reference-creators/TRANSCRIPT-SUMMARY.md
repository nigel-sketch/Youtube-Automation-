## Themes

### Theme 1 — SEO tutorials (Google Search Console, keywords, backlinks)
Videos: #7, #8, #11, #15, #25, #27, #29, #30, #31, #33, #34, #37, #43, #44, #45

**Common pattern:** Log into a free Google tool → follow 3–4 steps → rank higher or get more traffic. Very step-by-step. Often ends with "prompt in comments" or "save this."

**Best titles:** "10x your website traffic for free", "FORCE Google to rank your site", "Steal your competitor's SEO strategy"

### Theme 2 — AI tool tutorials (ChatGPT, NotebookLM, Claude, Gemini)
Videos: #1, #3, #6, #10, #13, #17, #18, #23, #28, #35, #39, #41, #47

**Common pattern:** AI tool X has a feature most people don't know about → here's the exact prompt → paste it → get a specific output. Often framed as one AI "destroying" another.

**Best titles:** "Why is no one talking about this?", "The best ChatGPT prompt ever?", "I'm ditching ChatGPT omg"

### Theme 3 — Case studies with receipts
Videos: #2, #4, #26, #45

**Common pattern:** Open with exact traffic/revenue numbers → "here are the receipts" → show SEMrush or GA4 screenshot → then explain the method. The numbers do the persuasion before any tip is shared.

**Best titles:** "And he's fully booked", "He knows NOTHING about SEO", "Their sales grew 500%"

### Theme 4 — Local / Google Maps SEO
Videos: #9, #19, #20, #24, #40, #48

**Common pattern:** One specific GBP or Maps action (keyword descriptions, service descriptions, QR codes, directions signals) → usually takes under 5 minutes → "save this one."

**Best titles:** "Rank on Google Maps in 30 seconds", "This isn't cheating, it's just smart"

### Theme 5 — Competitor research and traffic stealing
Videos: #11, #21, #23, #27, #41, #44

**Common pattern:** Use a tool (SEMrush, Ubersuggest, ChatGPT Agent, pageaudit.com, sitemap compare) to reverse-engineer what's working for a competitor → take their keywords or backlinks.

**Best titles:** "Steal your competitor's SEO strategy", "Sorry competitors", "This works way too good"

### Theme 6 — Personal story / drama
Videos: #5, #36, #46, #50

**Common pattern:** Opens on an emotional or absurd moment → tells the story as narrative → lands on a business or marketing lesson. No step-by-step instructions. Highest share/comment potential.

**Best titles:** "The Reddit hug of death", "I'm actually so pissed", "Do you remember this ad?", "They revolutionized everything"

## Hook patterns (spoken, first 3 seconds)

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Big number claim | "This guy's getting $40,000 of free website traffic every month" | Proof before ask — earns the next 30 seconds |
| AI disruption | "Google just destroyed Canva and ChatGPT" | Implies the viewer is already behind |
| Contradiction | "This guy makes $250k and knows NOTHING about SEO" | Violates expectation — brain needs to resolve it |
| Forbidden / illegal | "I'm definitely going to get in trouble for showing you this" | Flatters viewer: "you're getting insider knowledge" |
| Direct question | "Here's how to rank higher on Google Maps" | Clear promise, no mystery needed |
| Emotional reaction | "I'm actually so pissed" / "I'm obsessed" | Personal emotion creates parasocial pull |
