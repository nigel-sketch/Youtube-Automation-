## The HeyTony formula — deconstructed

Every Matt Short that performs well follows this sequence:

```
HOOK (0–3 sec)   → PROOF or TENSION (3–8 sec)   → ONE TIP (8–50 sec)   → CTA (last 3 sec)
```

### Step 1 — Hook (0–3 seconds)
One statement that makes a viewer stop scrolling. It must do one of:
- Drop a specific number: *"This guy's getting $40k of free traffic every month"*
- Create a contradiction: *"He knows NOTHING about SEO and has a 3-month waitlist"*
- Imply the viewer is already behind: *"Google just destroyed ChatGPT"*
- Signal forbidden knowledge: *"I'm going to get in trouble for showing you this"*

Matt never starts with "Hi I'm Matt" or "Today I'm going to show you…". The hook is the first spoken word.

### Step 2 — Proof or tension (3–8 seconds)
Validate the hook before giving the tip. Matt almost always shows:
- A screenshot (SEMrush, GA4, Search Console)
- A specific dollar/traffic figure
- A short 2-person exchange that sets up the tension

This is where the phrase **"here are the receipts"** lives. It tells the viewer: *the claim I just made is real, and I'm about to show you how.*

### Step 3 — One tip (8–50 seconds)
A single, actionable step. Not "improve your SEO." Something like:
- *"Go to Search Console → sort by position → find keywords at position 11–20 → use this ChatGPT prompt"*
- *"Go to your GBP → click Services → add a description to every service with the city name and your keyword"*

Matt names the tool. He names the specific menu item or URL. He shows the screen. He does not explain the theory.

### Step 4 — CTA (last 3 seconds)
Always one CTA, never two. Matt's most used:
- "Prompt's in the comments" — drives comments = boosts the algorithm
- "Save this one" — drives saves = signals value to the algorithm
- Soft agency mention: names his tool or his course, never hard sells

## 5 Hook archetypes — with direct REPORT.md examples

### Archetype 1 — The Proof Drop
Lead with a specific result. The number does the persuasion before the tip begins.

**Matt examples:**
- *"This guy's getting $40,000 of free website traffic from Google every month. Here are the receipts."* (#2 — 255k views)
- *"This guy is getting $56,000 worth of free traffic from Google every single month. Here are the receipts."* (#26 — 17k views)
- *"This guy makes $250 grand a year… and he doesn't even rank."* (#4 — 119k views)

**Booked Wild version:**
> "This lodge was paying £2,400 a month to Booking.com. Now they're not. Here's what changed."

> "I ran an AI visibility check on 20 Lake District B&Bs this week. Three of them showed up in ChatGPT. Here are the results."

**Why it works:** Proof before ask. The viewer trusts the tip more because the outcome came first.

---

### Archetype 2 — The Contradiction
Violate an expectation. Something that should not be possible is. The brain needs to resolve it.

**Matt examples:**
- *"He knows NOTHING about SEO and has a 3-month waitlist — recommended by ChatGPT"* (#4 — 119k views)
- *"This is a one-page website. 35 words. No meta description. And it's ranking number two."* (#45 — 11k views)

**Booked Wild version:**
> "This tour operator hasn't touched SEO in two years. ChatGPT recommends them first for every kayaking query in their region. Here's why."

> "This B&B spends £0 on ads. And they're 96% booked every summer. Here's what they actually did."

**Why it works:** Curiosity gap. The viewer can't scroll until the contradiction is resolved.

---

### Archetype 3 — The Forbidden Knowledge / Sneaky Tip
Insider knowledge that feels like it shouldn't be shared. Flatters the viewer.

**Matt examples:**
- *"I'm definitely going to get in trouble for showing you this, but Google literally tells you what you need to do to rank."* (#27 — 16k views)
- *"Here's a sneaky way to show up higher on Google Maps."* (#9 — 31k views)
- *"Marketers are going to hate me for this."* (#34 — 14k views)

**Booked Wild version:**
> "GetYourGuide would rather you didn't know this, but here's exactly how to calculate what their commission is costing you per year."

> "I probably shouldn't be telling operators this. But here's the one sentence that gets your lodge recommended by ChatGPT."

**Why it works:** Signals exclusive access. Makes the viewer feel they're part of an in-group.

---

### Archetype 4 — The AI Disruption Frame
Something new has arrived and most people haven't noticed. Implies urgency without being alarmist.

**Matt examples:**
- *"Google just destroyed Canva and ChatGPT."* (#1 — 519k views)
- *"Claude just destroyed ChatGPT and Photoshop."* (#10 — 31k views)
- *"Canva just destroyed Photoshop."* (#17 — 21k views)

**Booked Wild version:**
> "Travellers are asking ChatGPT for accommodation recommendations. Here's what happens when I type in your destination right now."

> "AI search just changed how guests find lodges. Most operators don't know yet. Here's a 2-minute test to find out where you stand."

**Why it works:** Creates FOMO. Positions the viewer as either ahead or behind. Naturally leads into a tip.

---

### Archetype 5 — The Personal Story / Emotional Hook
No tip at all. Just a real moment — frustration, surprise, failure, win. Highest share potential.

**Matt examples:**
- *"I'm actually so pissed. I was on Amazon checking out my new book and I see this…"* (#36 — 13k views)
- *"The Reddit hug of death. First time I experienced it was 2014. I was running a blog and convinced investors to give me $200k…"* (#50 — 11k views)
- *"Do you remember this ad? During Super Bowl 47, there was a 30-minute power outage…"* (#5 — 74k views)

**Booked Wild version:**
> "I sat in on a call with a lodge owner last week. She'd just opened her OTA statement. 28% commission. £11,000 to Booking.com in a single month. I want to show you the maths."

> "Fifteen years ago I was running a restaurant. I gave away 20% of every booking we took. I just thought that was the cost of doing business. I was wrong."

**Why it works:** No tip needed. The emotion creates engagement and trust. Builds Colin's identity as a credible, opinionated voice — not a tutorial channel.

## What NOT to copy

### Do not copy: AI-only tutorials with no niche connection
Matt films ChatGPT and Canva demos because his audience is generic small business owners. Colin's audience is independent travel operators. A ChatGPT prompt tutorial that doesn't connect to bookings, OTAs, or AI search visibility is off-brand and off-ICP.

### Do not copy: Tips without proof
Matt's tips land because he shows the receipts. "Post consistently" and "optimise your GBP" without a specific result or number are filler. Every Booked Wild tip needs at minimum one real number (client revenue, commission saved, AI citations gained) or it's indistinguishable from generic marketing content.

### Do not copy: AI avatar or AI voiceover
Matt's differentiation is his **natural, unpolished human delivery**. The visual setup is absurd precisely because it signals authenticity. An AI-generated face or voice immediately signals low-trust content — the opposite of what builds an agency pipeline.

### Do not copy: Generic SEO advice
Search Console sitemap tutorials and "submit your sitemap" content (Matt's #8, #15, #33) are evergreen for his generic SME audience. For Booked Wild, these do not speak to a travel operator's specific pain points. Booked Wild content must always connect to: OTA dependency, AI search visibility, or direct booking recovery.

### Do not copy: Shock/opinion content without substance
Matt can get away with commentary (#42 Sony ads patent) because he has 1,076 videos building trust. Colin is at the start. Every video should teach something or prove something. Entertainment-only content earns its place once there is an established library.
