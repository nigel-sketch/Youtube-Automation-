## 3. On-camera voice guidance (informed by brand files)

### What the voice should be

- Direct, personal, grounded. Speak from the position of someone who has been inside the industry, not above it.
- Named specifics at all times: name the platform, name the stat and its source, name the destination.
- Short sentences. Conversational but purposeful.
- Evidence-led: cite the source when using a stat. "BrightLocal surveyed consumers in 2026 and found..." is the model.
- Anti-OTA fire where appropriate — but evidence-based, not rant-based.

### What to avoid

- Agency-speak: do not say "help," "innovative," "bespoke," "tailored," "seamless." [Source: Brand_Voice_Guide.md]
- Overpromise: do not guarantee AI rankings or specific booking outcomes.
- Over-polished delivery: natural and human is the stated goal (Colin's preference per CLAUDE.md).
- Generic review of the obvious: every talking point must deliver specific useful information.
- Mystical or nature-fantasy language: not appropriate for educational video content.

### Proof style

- Cite named sources verbally and on screen where possible.
- Only use stats from verified-claims.md or claim-bank.md (High or Safe with qualification status).
- For illustrative maths (Angle A): label clearly both verbally and on screen as "illustrative."

### CTA style

- One CTA per video.
- Use action verbs: "Run the Booking Loss Calculator at bookedwild.com" or "Book the £97 Deep Insight Audit."
- Do not use: "click here," "learn more," "get started," "find out more." [Source: Brand_Voice_Guide.md]
