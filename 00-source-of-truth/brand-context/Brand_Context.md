# Business Identity for Booked Wild

## Industry

Marketing agency for independent travel and hospitality experience providers across Europe — lodges, retreats, tour operators, experience providers, food and drink venues, short-term rentals. Productised services covering AI search visibility (GEO/AEO), direct booking infrastructure, AI-ready website rebuilds, and editorial content production. Operationally part of Nivo Digital, the parent agency founded in 2017.

## What the Business Does

Booked Wild does two things, structurally connected:

We get independent travel and hospitality operators recommended by AI search engines like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity — and we rebuild their direct booking infrastructure so the visibility translates into bookings they own, not bookings rented from OTAs.

The connection is the point. AI-mediated discovery is replacing OTA-mediated discovery for travel planning. Operators visible in AI search can move bookings from Booking.com, GetYourGuide, Viator, Airbnb, and Tripadvisor Experiences — where 15–30% commission disappears every month — to direct channels they control. Operators invisible in AI search stay locked into the OTA dependency cycle: pay platform commission for visibility, lose the guest relationship at checkout, repeat next season.

Five productised services with posted prices: Tripadvisor & GBP Optimisation (£395), AI Visibility Fix (£495), Embedded Itinerary (£1,200 + £99/mo), Direct Booking OS (£1,500 + £149/mo), and AI-Ready Website Rebuild (£4,500–7,500). Fed by a free SEOptimer audit and a £97 paid Deep Insight Audit. Delivered remotely from Bristol, UK and Levi, Finland with specialists across Europe.

# Ideal Customer or Audience

## Primary Audience

Independent travel and hospitality experience providers in Europe with annual revenue between £200k and £3m. Specifically: lodges and resorts with on-site hospitality (3–30 rooms), tour operators running guided experiences (1–10 staff), retreat hosts (yoga, wellness, adventure, creative), self-catering rental owners (cabins, cottages, chalets, glamping, holiday homes — 2–10 properties), experience providers (kayaking, foraging, husky, photography, food tours), and destination food and drink venues (gastropubs, boutique restaurants, cafés that draw travellers).

These are the underdogs of the travel industry. The solo guide. The family-run lodge. The culture bearer. The operator running something genuinely worth booking — and watching the platforms take a cut of every guest who finds them.

These operators typically:

- Already run direct booking via their own website and pay 15–30% commission to OTAs (Booking.com, Airbnb, GetYourGuide, Viator, Tripadvisor Experiences) on the rest
- Have a website built 3–8 years ago that's "fine but not winning"
- Spend £1,000–£8,000 a month on marketing across an inconsistent mix of social, ads, agency retainers, and ad hoc projects
- Get most of their bookings from a frustrating mix of OTA dependency, repeat guests, word of mouth, and inconsistent SEO
- Are actively worried about how AI search is changing their visibility but don't know what to do about it
- Have read the OTA contract clauses about rate parity and commission and felt the squeeze
- Buy productised services with fixed prices when the deliverable is concrete and the price is sub-£2k for a one-off
- Buy retainers when there's a clear monthly outcome (booking recovery, ongoing visibility, content production)

## Secondary Audience

Tourism boards, destination management organisations, and regional tourism partnerships looking for B2B research and benchmark data on AI search visibility and OTA dependency in independent travel. They don't buy productised services; they license research, commission destination-level visibility audits, or partner on regional initiatives. Smaller proportion of revenue but high credibility multiplier — being trusted by Visit Cornwall or Visit Lapland legitimises Booked Wild for individual operators in those regions.

## Biggest Pain Points

These are the unspoken questions every operator is carrying. Booked Wild's job is to answer them.

1. **"AI search is recommending competitors when travellers ask for accommodation, experiences, or restaurants in my region — and I don't show up at all."** The discovery layer is shifting and most operators don't yet see it.
2. **"I'm paying GetYourGuide / Viator / Booking.com 20–30% commission on bookings I should be getting directly, and the commission costs are eating my margin."** The structural cost of platform dependency is rising, not falling.
3. **"I've spent £5,000+ on websites and SEO that don't generate enquiries or bookings."** The vague "marketing strategy" approach has burned them before.
4. **"I don't know who to trust on AI visibility — every agency claims to do it, nobody can prove it works, and the buzzwords are exhausting."** Specificity and verifiability matter more than hype.
5. **"Generic AI tools and ChatGPT prompts produce content that sounds like every other lodge / tour operator / restaurant in my area."** Real voice, real photography, real local knowledge — these aren't optional.
6. **"I'm building my business on rented land. If Booking.com or Airbnb changes their algorithm or raises commission again, I'm exposed."** The dependence trap is real and operators feel it.

## How They Find & Pick Booked Wild

Discovery happens through:

- Cold email outreach during a destination launch (the primary channel at this stage)
- Referrals from existing clients in the same destination or category
- Organic search and AI search for queries like "AI visibility for tour operators," "travel marketing agency [destination]," "how to reduce GetYourGuide commission," or "direct booking strategy for independent lodges"
- LinkedIn content from Colin and the team
- Trade press coverage of the European Travel AI Visibility Index research project
- Direct visits to bookedwild.com after seeing the brand mentioned by another operator or in industry coverage

They evaluate Booked Wild by:

- Running the free AI visibility audit on the website and seeing what comes back
- Reading 2–3 case studies of operators in their category or region with quantified outcomes (AI citation screenshots + booking-mix shift data)
- Checking whether the agency itself appears in AI search for the queries it claims to optimise for (the dog-food test)
- Reading the founder's bio and existing track record at Nivo Digital
- Noting that prices are posted in plain text, not gated behind discovery calls
- Seeing the stance on OTAs — direct, evidence-led, not soft on the threat
- Booking either the £97 Deep Insight Audit (if they want depth) or a discovery call (if they want a conversation first)

The decision trigger is usually one of three moments: (a) they see a competitor mentioned by ChatGPT or Perplexity when they test a relevant query, (b) they receive a quarterly OTA commission statement that crystallises the cost of platform dependency, or (c) they're planning a website rebuild and realise traditional SEO advice doesn't address AI search.
