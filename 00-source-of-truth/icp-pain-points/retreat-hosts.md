# ICP: Retreat Hosts

**Status: STUB — sourced from Brand_Context.md only. Colin's full ICP file will replace/supplement this when shared.**

---

## Who they are

Retreat hosts running yoga, wellness, adventure, or creative retreats. Often sole operator or small team. Guests book in groups or as individuals for multi-day experiences. Revenue from retreat packages rather than nightly room rates. Annual revenue £200k–£3m.

[Source: Brand_Context.md]

## What they spend on marketing

£1,000–£8,000/month. Often a patchwork — Instagram, a mailing list, a website, maybe a retreat directory listing, and word of mouth. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## How they get bookings now

Direct via website or email, retreat directory platforms (BookRetreats, Retreat Guru, etc.), Instagram referrals, word of mouth. Some use Airbnb or Booking.com for accommodation-only listings. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

---

## Their six pain points

| # | Pain point | Booked Wild angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Not showing up when someone searches "yoga retreat in [region]" in ChatGPT or Perplexity | AI visibility fix — their retreat needs to be cited, not just listed |
| 2 | Paying 15–20% to retreat directory platforms on every booking | Platform commission framing — same structural problem as OTAs |
| 3 | Generic retreat content that sounds like every other wellness venue | Sensory specificity — real place, real host, real experience |
| 4 | No system for capturing guest emails, nurturing repeat bookings, or requesting reviews | Direct Booking OS — booking flow, automated follow-up, review requests |
| 5 | Guest relationship owned by the platform, not the host | Rented land / platform ownership — the OTA dependency argument applies here too |
| 6 | Vulnerable to platform algorithm changes or commission increases | Resilience narrative — direct booking as insurance, not just preference |

[Source: Brand_Context.md — pain points 1–6, adapted for retreat segment]
