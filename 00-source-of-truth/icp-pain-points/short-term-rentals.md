# ICP: Short-Term Rental Owners

**Status: STUB — sourced from Brand_Context.md only. Colin's full ICP file will replace/supplement this when shared.**

---

## Who they are

Self-catering rental owners running cabins, cottages, chalets, glamping, or holiday homes across Europe. Typically 2–10 properties. Annual revenue £200k–£3m. Often family-run or owner-operated.

[Source: Brand_Context.md]

## What they spend on marketing

£1,000–£8,000/month across an inconsistent mix of social, ads, agency retainers, and ad hoc projects. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## How they get bookings now

Heavy Airbnb and Booking.com dependency. Some direct booking via own website. Word of mouth and repeat guests. Inconsistent SEO. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

---

## Their six pain points

| # | Pain point | Booked Wild angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | AI search is recommending competitors when travellers search for their area — and they don't appear at all | AI visibility fix — get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI |
| 2 | Paying Airbnb 3% (host split-fee) or 14–16% (single-fee) on every booking they should own | Commission cost content — verified Airbnb fee data available |
| 3 | Website built 3–8 years ago that's "fine but not winning" — not generating enquiries | Direct booking infrastructure — booking flow rebuild |
| 4 | Don't trust agencies claiming AI visibility — no one can prove it works | Booked Wild publishes citation evidence and booking-mix shift data |
| 5 | Generic AI content makes their property sound like every other rental in the region | Real voice, real photography, real local knowledge — non-optional |
| 6 | Building on rented land — if Airbnb raises commission or changes algorithm, they're exposed | OTA dependency trap — rate parity clauses, platform ownership vs provider ownership |

[Source: Brand_Context.md — pain points 1–6]

---

## Content angles that work for this segment

- Commission maths: what one year of Airbnb fees actually costs
- Trust signals a direct booking website must have before guests will book off-platform
- How to ask guests to book direct next time (without breaking platform terms)
- Reviews as part of the direct booking path
