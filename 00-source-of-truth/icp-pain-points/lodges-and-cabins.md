# ICP: Lodges and Cabins

**Status: STUB — sourced from Brand_Context.md only. Colin's full ICP file will replace/supplement this when shared.**

---

## Who they are

Independent lodges and resorts with on-site hospitality. Typically 3–30 rooms. Includes woodland lodges, lakeside cabins, mountain retreats, glamping sites with lodge accommodation. Owner-operated or small management teams. Annual revenue £200k–£3m.

[Source: Brand_Context.md]

## What they spend on marketing

£1,000–£8,000/month. Often includes a mix of Booking.com listing fees, social media spending, and a website that hasn't been rebuilt in years. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## How they get bookings now

Booking.com, Airbnb, and direct. Some use channel managers. Strong repeat guest and word-of-mouth component. May also rely on regional tourism association listings. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

---

## Their six pain points

| # | Pain point | Booked Wild angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Not showing up in AI search when travellers ask "best lodge in [region]" | AI visibility fix — schema, llms.txt, entity density in website copy |
| 2 | Paying 15–18% Booking.com commission on bookings they should own | Commission cost content — Booking.com rate unverified, use with qualification |
| 3 | Spent £5,000+ on a website and SEO that don't generate enquiries | Direct booking infrastructure — booking flow, not just traffic |
| 4 | Seeing competitors appear in ChatGPT recommendations while they don't | Decision trigger A — a competitor appearing in AI search is the moment operators act |
| 5 | Feeling squeezed by rate parity clauses in OTA contracts | Rented land / platform ownership framing |
| 6 | OTA algorithms changing without notice — occupancy drops with no explanation | Resilience narrative — own your bookings, own your future |

[Source: Brand_Context.md — pain points 1–6]

## Content angles that work for this segment

- What "your competitor shows up in ChatGPT and you don't" actually means commercially
- The real cost of Booking.com dependency for a 10-room lodge over 12 months (illustrative maths)
