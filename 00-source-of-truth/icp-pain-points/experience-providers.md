# ICP: Experience Providers

**Status: STUB — sourced from Brand_Context.md only. Colin's full ICP file will replace/supplement this when shared.**

---

## Who they are

Independent experience providers running activity-based offerings — kayaking, foraging, husky safaris, photography workshops, food tours, and similar. Often sole operator or 2–3 person team. Revenue per experience is relatively low; volume and repeat matter. Annual revenue £200k–£3m.

[Source: Brand_Context.md]

## What they spend on marketing

£1,000–£8,000/month. Often heavily dependent on GetYourGuide or Viator for discovery, supplemented by Instagram and local tourism listings. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## How they get bookings now

GetYourGuide, Viator, Tripadvisor Experiences. Some direct via website. Strong word of mouth and repeat — guests who loved the experience refer friends. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## Their six pain points

| # | Pain point | Booked Wild angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Not appearing in AI search when travellers ask for experiences in their region | AI visibility fix — this is the sharpest application for experience providers |
| 2 | Commission eroding margin on low-ticket experiences (20–30% on a £60 kayaking session is significant) | Commission maths content — illustrative maths, label clearly |
| 3 | No guest relationship after the experience — the platform owns the contact | Direct Booking OS — post-experience email capture, review request, rebooking flow |
| 4 | Reviews on OTA platforms don't feed a direct booking path | Reviews as direct booking trust signals — BrightLocal data applies |
| 5 | Website exists but doesn't book — visitors go back to GetYourGuide to pay | Direct booking flow rebuild — the website must close the booking |
| 6 | Platform commission increasing over time with no ability to negotiate | Rented land / platform ownership — the long-term dependency trap |
