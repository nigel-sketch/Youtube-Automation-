# ICP: Food and Drink Venues

**Status: STUB — sourced from Brand_Context.md only. Colin's full ICP file will replace/supplement this when shared.**

---

## Who they are

Destination food and drink venues that attract travellers — gastropubs, boutique restaurants, travel-draw cafés. Not high street chains. The kind of place that appears on regional food tourism itineraries. Annual revenue £200k–£3m. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## What they spend on marketing

£1,000–£8,000/month. Often a mix of Instagram, Google My Business, local food press, and Tripadvisor management. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## How they get bookings now

Direct reservations via phone and email. OpenTable, Resy, or similar for covers. Tripadvisor Experiences for food experiences (tasting menus, cooking classes). Word of mouth and regional tourism listings. [Source: Brand_Context.md]

## Their six pain points

| # | Pain point | Booked Wild angle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Not appearing when travellers ask AI "best gastropub in the Cotswolds" or "food experiences in the Lake District" | AI visibility fix — structured copy, entity density, Google Business Profile optimisation |
| 2 | Tripadvisor Experiences commission on cooking classes or food tours | Platform dependency framing — same structural problem as OTAs |
| 3 | Google Business Profile not optimised for AI search — citations go to competitors | GBP and Tripadvisor Optimisation — £395 Booked Wild product |
| 4 | Reviews are on Tripadvisor, not feeding a direct booking or reservation path | Reviews as trust signals — BrightLocal data applies directly here |
| 5 | No way to build a guest relationship beyond "they liked us on Instagram" | Guest data and repeat visit nurture — email capture, follow-up sequences |
| 6 | Tourism discovery is shifting to AI — if they're invisible there, the summer influx doesn't find them | AI-mediated discovery — the structural argument for why this matters now |
