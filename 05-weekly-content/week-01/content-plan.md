## Research-informed recommended first angle

### Angle: What does one year of platform dependency cost you?

**Why this angle is stronger and safer than the previous version:**

- The previous angle ("The real cost of one year on Booking.com") named Booking.com and used their commission rates as hard stats. Booking.com's partner portal is entirely behind authenticated login — no public primary source URL was found. Using those rates as hard stats in a published post is a source risk.
- This version uses the same pain, the same structure, and the same CTA — but grounds the example in Airbnb's publicly verified fee data, which was confirmed directly from [airbnb.co.uk/help/article/1857](https://www.airbnb.co.uk/help/article/1857).
- The "platform dependency" framing is deliberately broader — it applies to any OTA, which makes the post relevant to all 6 ICP segments without locking us to one platform's unverified rate.
- Any illustrative maths must be labelled as illustrative (see note below).

**Verified source available for this angle:**

Airbnb Experiences fee: 20% — confirmed from Airbnb Help Centre, article 1857, checked 2026-05-22.
Airbnb single-fee host service fee: 14%–16% (typically 15.5%) — same source.

These are the only OTA commission figures currently cleared for use as hard stats. Booking.com, Viator, and GetYourGuide rates remain unverified from primary source and must not be used as hard stats until authenticated partner-account verification is available.

**Illustrative maths note:**

Any example calculation in this script (e.g. "if you turn over £X and Y% goes to platforms…") must be clearly labelled as illustrative in the script and in the caption. Do not present illustrative maths as a real client result unless it is based on a verified Booked Wild client case study with explicit permission.

**Suggested hook (to refine with Colin):**

> "Here's what one year of platform dependency actually costs an experience operator."

Or:

> "Your platform statement just came in. Let's do the maths — illustrative, but honest."

**Suggested structure (60–90 seconds):**

1. Hook — name the pain: platform fees eating margin year after year (3 seconds)
2. Show the maths — use Airbnb Experiences 20% fee as the verified example; frame as illustrative for a typical operator (20–30 seconds)
3. Name what that money could fund instead — one or two specifics (20 seconds)
4. The alternative exists — direct booking infrastructure (10 seconds)
5. CTA — run the free Booking Loss Calculator at bookedwild.com (5 seconds)

**ICP:** Any of the 6 segments. Experience providers and STR operators who use Airbnb will feel the Airbnb example most directly.

**Voice mode:** Anti-OTA Advocate into Commercial Guide. No movement language needed.

**Supporting post angle:** The maths as a simple graphic or text post. Label any figures as illustrative. Link to the Booking Loss Calculator.
