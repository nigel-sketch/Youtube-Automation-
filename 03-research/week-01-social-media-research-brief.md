## 3. Colin voice and filming guidance

Informed by: Brand_Voice_Guide.md, Content_Standards.md, Founder Story as Trust Signal notebook (9d5e3fb7).

### Tone (what it should be)

- Storytelling Host with Anti-OTA fire. [Source: Brand_Voice_Guide.md — channel tone guide for LinkedIn, same applies to video]
- Direct, personal, opinionated, evidence-led. Speak from the position of someone who has been inside hospitality, not above it.
- Short sentences. Conversational but purposeful. No filler.
- UK English — favour, recognise, optimisation.
- Named specifics at all times: name the platform, name the stat source, name the destination.

### Tone (what to avoid)

- No agency-speak. Do not say "we help," "innovative," "bespoke," "tailored," "seamless." [Source: Brand_Voice_Guide.md — banned words list]
- No mystical or nature-fantasy language in educational videos. Sensory language is reserved for manifesto content, not educational Shorts.
- No overpromise on AI rankings. Use "increase the likelihood," "build the citation surfaces," not "guarantee."
- No generic review of the obvious. Every point must deliver a specific piece of useful information.
- Avoid over-polished delivery. HeyTony's appeal (as described by Colin) is natural and human — not corporate presentation.

### Filming setup (practical guidance only — not confirmed by Colin)

- Direct-to-camera. Minimal props. No teleprompter visible.
- Clean background or contextually relevant (a real hospitality setting, not a generic office).
- Landscape format preferred for YouTube; vertical for Shorts and Instagram Reels repurposing.
- Natural lighting where possible (matches HeyTony style reference).
- Colin's Levi Finland location is a strong differentiator if he films outdoors or with a Nordic backdrop — no other UK travel marketing agency has this setting.

Note: Filming setup must be confirmed by Colin. Do not prescribe equipment or location until he confirms what he has available.

### Delivery style

- Conversational and grounded. As if explaining to a friend who runs a lodge.
- Pause after the hook. Let the first 3 seconds breathe.
- Proof delivered plainly — "BrightLocal surveyed consumers in 2026 and found that…" — not in a hype-laden way.
- If using illustrative maths (Angle A), label it clearly on screen and verbally: "This is illustrative."

### Proof style

- Cite named sources on screen if possible (text overlay or spoken reference): "BrightLocal, 2026" is the gold standard.
- Do not use unverified stats. If a stat is not in verified-claims.md, do not use it in the script.
- Use Airbnb's publicly verified fee data for Angle A. Do not reference Booking.com, Viator, or GetYourGuide commission rates as hard stats until verified.

### CTA style

- One CTA per video. Do not stack multiple CTAs.
- Use action verbs: "Run the Booking Loss Calculator at bookedwild.com" or "Book the £97 audit."
- Do not use: "click here," "learn more," "get started." [Source: Brand_Voice_Guide.md — CTA Verb Library]

### Voice-note/interview workflow

Parked as a possible future option. Not to be actioned at this stage. Colin has not indicated this is his preference. Manual-first approach means a voice note or conversation with Colin to capture a natural script could be explored once the first manual cycle is complete. Not in scope for Week 01.
