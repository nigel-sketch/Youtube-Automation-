# Claim Bank

Full source-traced record of every claim in the research base.

Columns: Claim | Source | Source type | Confidence | Public-use status | Safer wording

## Source types

- **Primary** — original study, official platform documentation, direct observation
- **Internal** — Booked Wild source-of-truth file (facts about Booked Wild itself)
- **NotebookLM / secondary** — traced to a named source inside a notebook, but that source is itself secondary (trade blog, agency content, aggregator)
- **NotebookLM / academic** — traced to a named academic paper or peer-reviewed study inside a notebook
- **AI synthesis** — generated by AI without a traceable source document
- **Unverified** — origin unclear or source inaccessible

## Public-use status

- **Safe** — primary or internal source, stat confirmed, wording verified
- **Safe with qualification** — real source but requires "research suggests" or "studies indicate" framing, or the source is secondary
- **Not safe yet** — source inaccessible, contradicted, too old, or origin is AI synthesis
- **Do not use** — source actively contradicts the claim as stated

## Confidence levels

- **High** — primary or internal source, recently verified
- **Medium** — secondary or academic source, traceable but not independently confirmed
- **Low** — unverified, inaccessible, contradicted, or AI-generated without traceable origin

## Rules

- Do not use any claim at Hard stat confidence unless source type is Primary or Internal.
- Do not reference Ahrefs or SEMrush by name in published content.
- If confidence is Low, either find a better source or cut the claim entirely.
- Every stat used in a published post must have a source note in the draft file.
